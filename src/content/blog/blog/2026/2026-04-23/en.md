April 22 — I’ve truly entered the exam season. This year feels different from previous years, as I’ve already received an ED offer. !!For applicants to U.S. universities, exams are just one of the requirements for graduation, so they don’t feel that important to me personally. !!However, life doesn’t seem any more relaxed. Countless things are entangling me, so I’m writing this to sort through my recent thoughts.

## About Graduation

![Senior Year Music Carnival](./2026-04-23-img01.jpg "Casual shot from the senior music carnival")

After the farewell event on May 22, all senior students will leave campus. At the time of writing this, there is exactly one month left. There’s still planning to do for the farewell event and graduation ceremony. I’m not sure whether my plan for filming a graduation ceremony “signature shot” will work out.

Earlier this year, I switched from a Nikon D750 to a Sony A7M5. After changing systems, the improved video capabilities made me start shooting dynamic footage more frequently. As someone who often watches CS tournaments, I have a special attachment to “signature shots,” and I hope to leave behind such a meaningful piece of footage at the end of high school.

For the signatures, I plan to customize three high-transparency acrylic boards and use a magic arm for filming. Before the graduation ceremony, I plan to purchase a Viltrox 14mm f/4.0 lens for this purpose. My usual 24–105mm lens is a bit too long for this type of shot, and its 0.3m minimum focusing distance forces me to use a 9-inch or even larger magic arm. In contrast, this Viltrox lens not only provides the short focal length I need, but also has a focusing distance just over 10cm. This means a 7-inch magic arm should be sufficient, greatly improving portability. Surprisingly, a brand-new one can be found on Xianyu for only 850 RMB — I can’t think of a better option.

## About University

During the first half of senior year, I was too busy with studies to deal with the complicated matters related to NYU. After finishing the academic writing placement test, I put everything aside. Only after mock exams did I realize that F1 visa interview appointments at the U.S. Embassy in Beijing had already been booked until mid-July. I quickly filled out the DS-160 to prepare for the interview. Health documentation still requires medical checks and vaccinations, all of which need to be completed during exam season.

As for my expectations of university, I hope New York matches the version of it in my mind. The last time I visited New York was in 2018. After that, my two high school study trips to the U.S. were both in California. The first year at UCLA went smoothly and I earned a 4. The second year at UC Berkeley was tougher — I almost failed one course >_<. CS61C was indeed quite challenging for me at the time, and the tight schedule of the summer session didn’t give me enough time to review outside of the limited lab sessions. Hopefully, my EE studies at NYU will go more smoothly.

## What I’ve Been Busy With Recently

There are a lot of things I want to do lately — exploring university life, learning trading, playing CS, and more.

### Exploring NYU

NYU 2030 has set up an official Discord server. Most of the active users are international students, while Chinese students still prefer using WeChat. After the January orientation, most discussions in the group have been about school information and finding roommates.

My engineering school, Tandon, is located in Brooklyn, and my dorm is only a two-minute walk from campus. That might be one of the advantages of being in Brooklyn. Starting from sophomore year, I might move out.

The dorm layout is somewhat similar to what I saw at UC Berkeley, but our apartment includes a kitchen. Two people per room, two rooms sharing one bathroom — it’s a pretty good setup.

### The Trading Market

The market has remained unstable recently, with most assets moving sideways. Fortunately, I’m not holding spot positions at the moment and am still learning.

ETHUSDT has been fluctuating around 2250–2400. Some friends recently made profits by trading within Ethereum’s range structure. BTCUSDT continues to be influenced by international developments. The opening and closing of the Strait of Hormuz affected BTC, which in turn influenced overall crypto market sentiment. After consolidating for dozens of hours, BTC surged to 77,000 USDT when the strait reopened. However, the situation reversed quickly — when the strait closed again, I received a notification at 9 PM on April 19 that BTC had dropped below 74,000 USDT. This pattern repeated twice. At the time of writing, BTC’s isolated high has reached 79,500, just $500 away from returning to the 80,000 level. Thinking back to just a month ago when people were debating whether to add positions at 60,000 or 74,000, looking at current prices makes it easier to accept.

## Conclusion

Adjusting my state of mind recently is very important. I need to figure out what I want to do and what I should be doing, and not waste too much time. At this stage, I feel somewhat trapped by what seem like “important matters.” I hope to break out of this cycle. Play games well, learn trading well, and take good photos. Keep moving forward — the break is just ahead.
